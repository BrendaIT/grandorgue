Getting Started

Introduction

MyOrgan allows the user to load and play virtual pipe organs. Using sample sets, MyOrgan is able to combine the individual pipe sounds and organ behaviors to simulate a variety of pipe organs.


Sample Sets

MyOrgan is capable of loading and faithfully reproducing a variety of sample sets. Through extensive testing, it appears highly compatible with definition files that have worked in Hauptwerk™ version 1. Other types of sample sets may also be compatible, and other formats may soon be available. Some sources of compatible sample sets, including freely available ones, are listed on the MyOrgan home page. As such, you won't be able to do anything at all with MyOrgan without at least one sample set. Sample sets generally come packaged or with installers. By default, the software loads organs from "My Organs" in your documents folder, but you may store sample sets wherever is convenient and space permits.


Features

  • High definition output with supporting hardware
  • Optimized MMX® core for extraordinary polyphony
  • High precision internal processing for optimal sound quality
  • Polyphony management to avoid CPU overload
  • Low latency output with proper hardware support
  • Quick and easy MIDI setup, including "Listen for Event"
  • Lossless compression reduces RAM requirements by up to 40%
  • Release sample enhancements for realistic sound
  • All features and enhancements configurable
  • High speed loading and caching of sample sets
  • Save settings per sample set or export to settings files

Requirements

All Platforms

  • Pentium® II equivalent, or better
    NOTE: A newer, faster processor will permit a higher polyphony
  • Sufficient RAM to fully load the largest sample set you wish to use
  • A hard disk (or other storage) with enough free space to store sample sets

Microsoft® Windows®

  • Windows® 2000, XP, or later
    NOTE: Older versions are not supported, although the software may function properly
  • Sound card supporting DirectSound® and/or ASIO™ (recommended)
  • MIDI interface device or loopback driver
